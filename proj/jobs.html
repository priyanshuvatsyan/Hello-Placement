1.Software Developer/Engineer

Software development roles are prevalent for CS graduates. They involve designing, coding, testing, and maintaining software applications. Popular programming languages and technologies in demand include Java, Python, JavaScript, and related frameworks.

2.Web Developer

Web development roles focus on building websites and web applications. Knowledge of HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and front-end and back-end frameworks is valuable

3.Data Analyst/Scientist:

Data-related roles involve collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data to provide insights. Skills in data analysis tools, statistical programming languages (e.g., Python, R), and data visualization are important.

4.Quality Assurance (QA) Engineer:

QA engineers are responsible for testing software and ensuring its quality. They design test cases, perform testing, and report defects.

5.Cloud Engineer:

With the growing adoption of cloud technologies, cloud engineers manage and optimize cloud infrastructure and services from providers like AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.

6.Cybersecurity Analyst:

In an era of increasing cyber threats, cybersecurity analysts work to protect systems and data from security breaches. They analyze threats and implement security measures.

7.Mobile App Developer:

Mobile app developers create applications for smartphones and tablets. Knowledge of iOS (Swift) or Android (Java/Kotlin) development is essential.

8.DevOps Engineer:

DevOps engineers focus on automating and streamlining software development and deployment processes. They work on continuous integration and continuous delivery (CI/CD) pipelines.

9.Database Administrator (DBA):

DBAs manage and maintain databases, ensuring data integrity and availability. SQL expertise and knowledge of database management systems (e.g., MySQL, PostgreSQL) are crucial.

10.Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) Engineer:

AI and ML engineers work on developing AI/ML models and applications. Proficiency in machine learning libraries and frameworks like TensorFlow and PyTorch is valuable.

11.Full Stack Developer:

Full stack developers have expertise in both front-end and back-end development, allowing them to work on the entire software development stack.

12.IT Support Specialist:

IT support specialists assist users with technical issues, troubleshoot problems, and provide IT support within organizations.

13.Business Analyst:

Business analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They gather requirements, analyze data, and help in the decision-making process.

14.Project Manager:

Project managers oversee software development projects, ensuring they are completed on time and within budget. They require both technical and managerial skills.

15.Freelancing/Remote Work:

Many computer science professionals in India also explore freelancing opportunities or remote work, especially in the fields of web development, mobile app development, and content creation.
